“Arrows of Plague” (2023)
16mm, 4:3, color, 3'33 (loop)
“Virus” literally means slimy juice in Latin. It has been understood as something that flows, formless but powerful. Thinking about the invisible (or microscopic) often depends on figurative language, magnifying it to a more human scale, making it possible to be seen beyond the laboratory. Before the modern concept of a virus emerged, there were different ways to understand the causes of various mass infections. Think of Apollo or his twin sister Artemis – they were holding bows and had quivers of arrows slung over their shoulders. At least, that is how the Apollonian wrath was imagined in antiquity: the gods shooting infected arrows only to provide the cure of them later.
